As a tax professional, you are required to ask questions, evaluate if the return makes sense, reconstruct income if needed, and document everything. This method walks you through each step — confidently.
Step 1

Business Identity

  • What type of work did you do?
  • How did you get paid? (cash, Zelle, 1099…)
  • How often did you work?
  • One client or many?
Confirms it's a real business — your #1 due diligence obligation.
Step 2

Income Reconstruction

  • How many clients per week?
  • What did you charge per job?
  • How many months did you work?
  • Any bank statements or logs?
Multiply it out. Don't just accept "I made $X" — build the number together.
Step 3

Expense Discovery

  • What did you buy for the business?
  • Did you drive for work? How often?
  • Any apps, licenses, or fees paid?
  • Did you use part of your home?
Every business has expenses. If they say none — dig deeper.
Step 4

Reasonableness Test

  • Can this income support their lifestyle?
  • Are the numbers suspiciously round?
  • Are expenses too low or too high?
  • Does this suspiciously maximize EITC?
Pause and ask: does this story make sense as a whole?

Red flags to watch for

Very round income numbersZero reported expensesCash-only businessCan't describe the workIncome maximizes EITC exactlyNo receipts or recordsInconsistent storyUnrealistic income for work type
